once youve got it ready, how do i get it to my phone?
Claude

You've got two ways to do it, with very different amounts of work. I'd start with #1 today because it takes 10 minutes, then do #2 in parallel.

Option 1 — PWA, on your phone in 10 minutes (today)

This gets the prototype itself onto your home screen, icon and all. It's not the App Store version, but it looks and feels like an app and updates instantly when we change anything.

What you need:

A way to host the HTML file so your phone can open it at a URL. Easiest: Netlify Drop (netlify.com/drop) — literally drag the project folder onto the page, get a URL like hit-zero-abc.netlify.app.

Steps:

I'll bundle Hit Zero Web.html and all its dependencies into a single folder you can drop onto Netlify.
You drag → get a URL → text it to yourself.
On iPhone: open the URL in Safari (not Chrome — only Safari can install PWAs on iOS) → tap Share → Add to Home Screen.
On Android: Chrome → menu → Install app.

You'll have a Hit Zero icon on your home screen that opens fullscreen with no browser chrome. It's live within the hour.

Say the word and I'll build the deployable bundle right now.

Option 2 — Real App Store build (the right way)

This is the one with the native wrapper (Capacitor), the real Supabase backend, auth, video recording, push notifications. Everything I already scaffolded in hit_zero_client/ and hit_zero_backend/.

What you need from you:

A Mac with Xcode (can be a rental — MacInCloud is ~$30/mo, or hire a contractor for the first build)
Apple Developer account — $99/year, sign up at developer.apple.com (24–48 hr approval)
Google Play Developer account — $25 one-time at play.google.com/console
A domain — hitzero.app or similar (~$15/year, Cloudflare or Namecheap)

The work — two paths:

2a. You hire a React Native / Capacitor dev (what I'd recommend) The project is in a state where a mid-level mobile developer can take hit_zero_client/ + hit_zero_backend/ + HANDOFF.md and ship to TestFlight in about a week. Cost: somewhere between $3k–8k for the initial ship depending on who. Post on toptal.com, fiverr pro, or ask around in cheer gym groups — you'd be surprised who's in there.

2b. You do it yourself on a Mac Everything is laid out in HANDOFF.md. Realistic timeline if you've never done iOS dev: 2 weekends to first run on your phone via Xcode, 1 more weekend to TestFlight, 1 more weekend to submit. About 40 hours total.

What happens after that:

Week 1: supabase start → local backend running, point the client at it
Week 2: your phone is running a dev build via USB or QR code
Week 3: TestFlight beta — invite your coaches and 3–5 parents
Week 4: iterate based on what they say
Week 5–7: App Store submission + review (2–3 weeks for Apple, 1 week Google)
Week 8: live in both stores
My recommendation

Do both at once:

Today: Let me build the PWA bundle → you drag-and-drop to Netlify → app on your phone tonight. Gets you + 1 or 2 trusted people test-driving it immediately. Free, no accounts needed.
This week: Register the Apple + Google developer accounts (they take 1–2 days to approve so start now). Buy the domain.
Next week: Either hire a dev or start the Xcode path yourself.

The PWA version will keep working even after you ship to the stores — think of it as the "web version" that'll always exist alongside the native apps.

Want me to build the drag-and-drop PWA bundle right now? Or do you want to talk through the Apple Developer signup and domain first?
